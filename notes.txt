So i'm thinking that different archetypes respond differently to different responses. It also depends on their inner state.

An Arc can be defined by definining:
- actors (select a specific NPC or any NPC with a given archetype, maybe) - defines what NPCs are going to be affected by the decisions
- conditions (of the player state or NPC state)
- each arc is defined by three stages (3 separate scenarios):
  1. setup
  2. first problem
  3. consequence
  4. resolution
- each stage decision influences the following stages as well as the long-running NPC states
- between-arc incluence is materialized only via long-running NPC states

Decisions have defined tags:
- helpful
- ambitious
- honest
- political
- boundary
- loyal
- confrontational
- credit-taking
- team-first
- boss-pleasing

It is defined beforehand how different archetypes react to different labels (how their stats change). One can continously add to this vocabulary of labels + reactions.

Thanks to this design, the individual scenarios almost don't matter, what matters is the decision labels. The actual decisions matter only within the context of a single Arc.


---

The player has stats:
- reputation
- political capital
- stress

Each NPC has stats:
- warmth
- respect
NPCs also have archetypes, these determine how they are affected by different Decision Tags as well as in which Arc the NPC can be an Actor (and which actor).

The Archetypes are:

The Ladder Climber

Obsessed with optics and advancement. Responds well to anything that makes them look good by association, poorly to anything that threatens their position. High sensitivity to ambitious and political tags, essentially immune to honest ones.
The Mentor

Genuinely invested in developing people, but has strong opinions about the right way to do things. Rewards honesty and competence signals, suspicious of political maneuvering. The one archetype where honest is always the safe play.
The Bureaucrat

Risk-averse, process-obsessed, deeply uncomfortable with confrontation or deviation. Rewards boss-pleasing and loyal, punishes confrontational and ambitious hard. Not malicious — just captured by the system.
The Saboteur

Superficially collegial, covertly competitive. Uniquely sensitive to credit-taking — mirrors it back. Warmth is decoupled from Respect almost completely; they'll smile and undermine simultaneously.
The Burnout

Checked out, cynical, low energy. Low sensitivity across almost all tags — hard to move either direction. The exception is boundary tags, which they respond to with unexpected warmth. Basically a low-stakes NPC unless the player is also burning out, in which case the arc gets dark.

The main storytelling unit is an Arc. An Arc has 4 stages:
Stage 1 — Setup      : scene-setting, no decision yet. NPC initiates contact.
Stage 2 — Dilemma    : first real decision. Tags fire, stats update.
Stage 3 — Escalation : consequences of stage 2 manifest + a second decision.
                        This is where it gets uncomfortable.
Stage 4 — Aftermath  : no decision. Short resolution text that reflects
                        cumulative choices. May emit canonical events.
Each Arc is defined as:
- actors:
  - canonical names for any NPC that fits given conditions

NPCs that are actors in an Arc are locked and cannot become actors in another Arc. At any time, at most 2-3 Arcs can be active.

Intra-Arc consequences can be closely modeled by the definition of Arc scenarios. Within an Arc, more complicated logic that is dependent specifically on previous same-Arc decisions -- this allows for more complex storyline flows.

Extra-Arc consequences are handled via each decision affecting cretain NPC's stats. 
An atomic unit of gameplay is a scenario -- given a specific context, the user is tasked to choose 1 out of 4 decisions. Each decision has different tags.

Tags are the NPC and player stats primitives. A Tag is two vectors - an archetype effect (each component is a scalar of how much is an archetype affected by the tag, has direction and magnitude), vice versa for the player stats vector. 
The Tags are:
- helpful

- honest
